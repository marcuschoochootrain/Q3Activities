What are Earthquake Drills? Why do we need to learn about them?

Earthquake drills are an opportunity to practice how to be safer during earthquakes: "Drop, Cover and Hold On." Also, to update emergency plans and supplies, and to secure your space in order to prevent damage and injuries.

Preocedure Of An Earthquake Drill

Images Steps Explanations
Drop DROP where you are, onto your hands and knees. This position protects you from being knocked down and reduces your chances of being hit by falling or flying objects.
Cover your head and neck with one arm and hand. If a sturdy table or desk is nearby, crawl underneath for shelter. If no shelter is nearby, crawl next to an interior wall. Stay on your knees; bend over to protect vital organs.
Hold On until the shaking stops. Under shelter: hold on to it with one hand; be ready to move with your shelter if it shifts No shelter: hold on to your head and neck with both arms and hands.
